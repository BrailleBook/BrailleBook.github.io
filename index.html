About Us

Braille Book Nonprofit Organization for the blind is a nonprofit organization founded in August 2020. Our mission is to create positive change and make a difference in the lives of the visually impaired group. We dedicate ourselves to volunteer services such as Braille translation, blind tutoring, and blind offline guidance. Within 3 years, our organization has expanded to over 500 voluntary members. We have helped over 50 visually impaired high school or college student complete their studies and translated over 150 textbooks, novels, or academic materials from English to Braille...

Our Services

We provide a wide range of services. There are numerous ways to get involved. You can volunteer your time in translating books to Braille, connecting with local schools for the blind, tutoring visually impaired students who seek for academic guidance, or even learning Braille in your free time and reach those who need it most.
Our voluntary translation program translates books from English or other languages into Braille based on the needs or interests of the blind community we serve. Our volunteers are usually responsible for proofreading electronic scans of books in their original language. Then, our specialized Braille translators translate these electronic books into Braille and deliver them to the visually impaired community. Attempting to meet the needs of the entire blind community as much as possible, we translate a wide variety of books, including medical texts, textbooks, fictional novels, and more.



Recently, we are developing an AI English-to-Braille Translator through the deep learning models. By taking a picture of some Braille texts, the AI translator is able to recognize each Braille character and match it to its corresponding English letter, or vise versa. We trained our translator through a dataset of Braille characters. Based on our initial model which could only recognize letters, we then constructed the secondary model to generate sentences and paragraphs from these letters. Braille has nothing different from other languages. We aspire to provide equal opportunities for Braille learners to get access to the globe.

We offer an online tutoring program for our visually impaired students. The students we serve range from high school students to graduate students. Based on the subject or course requirements for which they need help, the organization matches them with their corresponding tutors. We have helped multiple high students enter their dream colleges and college students pursue their future careers.

All portrait photos uploaded on the website have been authorized by the person themselves.

There are times when our visually impaired population needs to travel, we provide them with transportation and guidance services. Our experienced volunteers usually would wait them at certain locations like bus or subway stations and escort the blind people to their destinations. Our volunteers show responsibility and care because they need to point out obstacles to the blind and understand them whenever they need help.

About This Website

This website is designed especially for the visually impaired group. Since most visually impaired people have problems with recognizing colors and lights' shading, it is critical to create high contrast text. Our website has passed the WCAG web accessibility test and received a level AAA in color contrast, which means a contrast ratio of at least 7:1. Our dark green theme background color creates a sharp contrast with the white text. In our body sections, we attempted to avoid background color under the text by simply applying white background and black text. We also make sure that our website conforms to the structural requirements, the ARIA references, etc.
To help the visually impaired people have better access to the content of our website, we incorporate photos and videos with concise text.
